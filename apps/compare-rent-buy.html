Input Guide

Simulation Period — How many years to project. Can be shorter or longer than the mortgage term — e.g. simulate 20 years of a 30-year mortgage.

Mortgage Term — The full length of the loan (e.g. 25 or 30 years). This sets the monthly repayment amount. If your simulation period is shorter, you'll see the remaining balance on the loan at that point.

Mortgage Rate — Annual interest rate on your loan (e.g. 3.5% = 0.035).

Rent Increase (%) — How much rent rises each year.

Home Appreciation (%) — Expected yearly increase in home value.

Market Return (%) — Expected annual return if you invest instead of tying up capital in a home.

Maintenance (%) — Yearly upkeep cost as % of home value.

Property Tax (%) — Annual property tax rate.

Fixed Buying Costs — One-time costs: notary, closing costs, taxes, etc.

Income & Raises — Monthly take-home and annual salary growth used to simulate savings over time.

Scenario Definitions

Rent Net Worth: You choose to rent a home. The money you would have spent on a down payment and buying costs, plus any leftover monthly income (income minus rent), is invested in the stock market.

Buy Net Worth: You buy a home. You pay the mortgage, property taxes, and home maintenance. Any leftover monthly income is invested in the stock market. Your Net Worth is your home equity plus your stock portfolio, minus initial buying costs.

Savings Account: You rent, but instead of investing in the stock market, all your savings go into a traditional savings account earning the fixed interest rate specified in the sidebar.

Cash (Do Nothing): You rent, but you keep all your extra money in cash under the mattress. It grows at 0%.
